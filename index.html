Facts About the Great Outdoors!

The Appalachian Mountains

The Appalachian Mountains are among the oldest mountain ranges in the world, estimated to be around 480 million years old. They stretch for about 1,500 miles from Newfoundland in Canada down to Alabama. Mt. Mitchell in North Carolina is the highest peak in the range at 6,684 feet.

Most Visited National Park

Great Smoky Mountains National Park, located on the border of North Carolina and Tennessee, is the most visited national park in the U.S., attracting over 12 million visitors annually. It's famous for its misty, "smoky" atmosphere and diverse wildlife.

Dinosaur Fossils in National Parks

Dinosaur National Monument, located on the Colorado-Utah border, contains one of the richest dinosaur fossil beds in the world. Visitors can see fossilized dinosaur bones embedded in rock.
